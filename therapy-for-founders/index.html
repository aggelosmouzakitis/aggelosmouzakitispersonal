Therapy for founders who built something that works and are now paying for it personally

The problem

You built the company. It is working. And somewhere along the way your identity became indistinguishable from its performance. A bad quarter feels like a personal failure. A good quarter buys temporary relief but never quite settles the question underneath. The loneliness deepens as the company grows, because the people around you are increasingly people you manage, not people you can be honest with.

The usual advice (get a coach, set boundaries, take a holiday) does not work when the problem is structural. The exhaustion is not from hours. It is from carrying weight that nobody around you sees or is equipped to help with.

Why founders need a different kind of therapist

The isolation is structural. The identity fusion is total. The pressure is multi-directional. A therapist who has never operated inside that environment will either treat it as generic stress or miss what is really happening underneath the performance.

Founders do not burn out the way employees burn out. The company is not just a job. It is an extension of self. When it struggles, you struggle. When it succeeds, you feel relief rather than joy. The question of who you are if you are not crushing it is not abstract for founders. It is the question that sits behind every decision, every sleepless night, every strained conversation at home.

How I work with founders

I am a licensed psychotherapist who spent 18+ years in B2B SaaS, including advising 50+ companies on growth. I know the language, the stakes, the specific way that founder life compresses personal and professional identity into a single point of failure.

Most of the founders I work with are dealing with performance dependence: a pattern where achievement has become the primary way they regulate their sense of worth. That pattern usually predates the company by decades, but founding a company gives it the perfect structure to run unchecked. We work with the root pattern, not just the current symptoms.

I write about this at Undisguised. The writing points at the patterns. The private work is where we move them.

Common questions

Why do founders need a different kind of therapist?

The isolation is structural. The identity fusion is total. The pressure is multi-directional. A therapist who has never operated inside that environment will either treat it as generic stress or miss what is really happening underneath the performance.

What do founders typically bring to therapy?

Isolation that deepens as the company grows. Identity that has become indistinguishable from the company's performance. Chronic decision fatigue. Relationship strain. Self-defeating patterns that create the exact problems they are trying to avoid. Burnout that rest does not fix because the source is emotional weight, not hours worked.

Can I do this while running a company?

Yes. Most founder clients do weekly or biweekly sessions remotely. The real question is whether the cost of not doing it (the reactive decisions, the strained relationships, the mounting internal pressure) is something your company can keep absorbing.

Is this completely confidential?

Yes. This is a private therapeutic relationship governed by professional ethics. Nothing is shared with anyone, ever.
