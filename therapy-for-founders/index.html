Therapy for founders who carry the weight of the company in their body

The founder problem

There is a specific isolation that shows up in founders that is distinct from loneliness. You are surrounded by people who depend on you: investors, employees, co-founders, customers. And precisely because they depend on you, none of them can be the person you are honest with about what it actually costs to hold all of it.

So you perform. You perform certainty in board meetings. You perform calm in all-hands. You perform optimism for your co-founder. And the gap between what you project and what you actually feel gets wider until it becomes the most exhausting part of the job.

This is not a coaching problem. This is not a "mental fitness" problem. This is a structural psychological burden that comes with the role, and it requires real therapeutic work to address.

What I see in founders

After years of working with founders both as a growth advisor and as a therapist, the patterns are consistent. Identity has become completely fused with the company. When the company is up, you are up. When it is down, you disappear into it. There is no stable sense of self that exists independent of the last metric you checked.

Decision fatigue has become chronic and is no longer about the decisions. It is about the weight of being the person who has to make them, knowing that other people's livelihoods depend on you getting it right. Overthinking becomes a permanent operating mode that feels productive but produces nothing except exhaustion.

Relationships suffer. Not because you do not care, but because there is nothing left after the company takes its share. And the worst part: you cannot talk about any of this without it being interpreted as weakness, doubt, or a signal that something is wrong with the business.

Common questions

Why do founders need a specific kind of therapy?

Because the psychological environment of founding is unique. The isolation is structural. The identity fusion is total. The pressure is multi-directional. A therapist who has never operated inside that environment will either treat it as generic stress or miss what is really happening underneath the performance.

What do founders typically bring to therapy?

Isolation that deepens as the company grows. Identity that has become indistinguishable from the company's performance. Chronic decision fatigue. Relationship strain. Self-defeating patterns that create the exact problems they are trying to avoid. Burnout that rest does not fix because the source is emotional weight, not hours worked.

Can I do this while running a company?

Yes. Most founder clients do weekly or biweekly sessions remotely. The real question is whether the cost of not doing it, the reactive decisions, the strained relationships, the mounting internal pressure, is something your company can keep absorbing.

Is this completely confidential?

Yes. This is a private therapeutic relationship governed by professional ethics. Nothing is shared with anyone, ever.
