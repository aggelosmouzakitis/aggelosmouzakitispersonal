I'm Aggelos Mouzakitis, a licensed psychotherapist and burnout coach for high-functioning people in tech. Based in Ireland, working globally.

I spent 18+ years inside B2B SaaS before training as a therapist. I know what high-performance environments do to people because I've lived inside the system that produces the problems I now help people solve.

What I do

I work with founders, executives, and senior tech professionals on the pattern that sits underneath most of what brings them to therapy: performance dependence. The point where achievement stops being drive and starts becoming regulation. Where your worth becomes contingent on output, and any interruption to that output (a bad quarter, a failed launch, a role change) threatens not just your career but your sense of self.

A lot of high-functioning burnout is not just overwork. It is a shame-driven relationship with achievement. Performance becomes identity, regulation, and proof of worth. That can create success for years, but it also creates fragility. When life stops going your way, everything built on that foundation starts to crack: motivation, relationships, health, the ability to feel like enough without the next win.

I help people keep their drive while ending the dependency. Failure, uncertainty, or a dip in performance should not collapse your relationships, your motivation, or your sense of self.

Who I work with

Senior tech professionals, founders, VPs, and executives who are successful by every external measure but are starting to notice the cost. The anxiety that doesn't match the accomplishments. The irritability that leaks into relationships. The sense that taking your foot off the gas, even slightly, would reveal something you are not ready to face.

I also work with people navigating imposter syndrome, career transitions, leadership isolation, and the broader identity questions that come with operating at a high level for a long time.

I write about the psychology of ambition, identity, and performance at Undisguised. The writing names what most people in high-stakes careers feel but rarely say out loud. The private work is where we actually change it.
