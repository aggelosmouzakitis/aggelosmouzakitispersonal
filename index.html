Therapy & executive coaching

I work with founders, executives, and senior tech leaders on the harder, less visible challenges that come with operating at a high level: the identity questions that success doesn't answer, the performance pressure that leaks into everything, the isolation that nobody around you is equipped to name.

Most therapists don't understand your world. I do. I've sat in the rooms where growth targets get set, where pricing gets debated, where the decision to cut a team happens in a Tuesday afternoon meeting. That context matters when someone walks into a session carrying what it actually costs to lead.

I write about the psychology of ambition, identity and performance at Undisguised, and work with senior professionals in 1:1 sessions to go deeper into what the essays can only point at. The writing names what most people in high-stakes careers feel but rarely say out loud. The private work is where we actually change it.

Why the duality matters

I'm a licensed psychotherapist who is also a working growth professional with deep B2B SaaS experience. In both roles I do the same thing: connect the right insights, spot the patterns others miss, and design strategies that actually work, whether for a product or a person.

The growth career isn't a second identity. It's the reason the therapy works. I don't need you to explain your context, your org dynamics, or what "series B pressure" feels like. I already know. That saves you time and lets us get to the real work faster.
